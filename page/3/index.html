Attentive Programming

by Paul Van Cleave | July 05, 2011

I’m currently reading Matthew Crawford’s ”Shop Class as Soulcraft” who writes about how manual labor can make you spiritually whole. To get us to look at work in new ways, he describes work using the categories: assertiveness and attentiveness. Crawford writes that work is not exclusively assertive or attentive and that to obtain the spiritualism that comes from manual work, you need balanced quantities of both. Too much assertiveness may mean you may destroy your work, while too much attentiveness may mean you could go out of business. Soulcraft is being able to apply just the right amount of each.
